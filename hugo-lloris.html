Hugo Hadrien Dominique Lloris (born December 26, 1986) is a French professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for Premier League side Tottenham Hotspur and leads the France national team. He has three times been named Ligue 1 Goalkeeper of the Year by the National Union of Professional Footballers (UNFP).

Hugo Lloris Biography

Hugo Lloris was born on December 26, 1986, to an upper-class family in the Mediterranean city of Nice. His French mother was a lawyer, while his father is a Spanish-born financier living in Monte Carlo. Gautier, his younger brother, was a central defender for his elder brother’s previous club OGC Nice. Lloris excelled in tennis as a child, similar to his international and club colleague Yoann Gourcuff, and played until the age of 13. Before deciding to concentrate on football, he was one of the best players in his age group, placing high in the country’s national rankings.

Lloris attended Nice’s Lycée Thierry Maulnier until 2004.

Career

  • Hugo Lloris has been equally successful for his native country’s senior football team as he has been for his club team. He represented France at various youth levels before earning his first senior team cap in a friendly against Uruguay in November 2008.
  • He was the team’s first-choice goalkeeper for the 2010 World Cup finals in South Africa, and he was named interim captain in November of that year. On February 28, 2012, he was named the first-choice captain.
  • France advanced to the quarter-finals of both the EURO 2012 and the 2014 World Cups under his leadership, but were eliminated by the eventual winners of both tournaments. He also led the team to the 2016 Euro finals, where they were defeated by Portugal. In a similar vein, he has led the team to the 2018 World Cup finals, where they hope to lift the coveted trophy for the first time in 20 years.
  • He debuted in 2018 against Brighton in a 1-1 draw. Hugo was the 61st player to reach milestones for the club.
  • Hugo played against PSV Eindhoven in the Champions League in 2018, and the game ended 2-2. In the year, he made his 300th appearance in the 2nd leg match against Manchester City, which ended in a 2-2 draw.
  • On October 5th, he suffered an elbow injury while playing against Tottenham Hotspur FC. He had surgery in November 2019 and returned to the field in January 2020 to face Norwich FC.
