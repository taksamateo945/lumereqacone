Kyle Pitts is a young American football tight end who played college football at the University of Florida. With impressive stats in his three seasons-long tenure at Florida, Kyle emerges as the top prospect in the upcoming NFL Draft.

Why Is Kyle Pitts Not Playing?

Kyle Pitts’s magical college career with the University of Florida makes him one of the hot prospects of the 2021 NFL Draft. He began his amateur career at Archbishop Wood Catholic High School and later committed to the University of Florida to play college football.

Following his three fruitful seasons with the Florida Gators football team, Kyle decided to not play anymore. But, don’t get confused! He didn’t quit his football career.

In fact, Kyle Pitts decided to forgo his senior year at Florida to enter the 2021 NFL Draft in December 2020. This year’s draft is set to take place from 30 April to 1st May and Kyle is already considered in the top five list of the bright TE (Tight Ends).

Kyle Pitts Florida Stats, Injury

The bright star joined the University of Florida in 2018. In his true freshman season, he played in 11 games and recorded three receptions for 73 yards and a touchdown. Eventually, he played in all 13 games in his sophomore season in 2019 and registered the impressive stats of 54 receptions for 649 yards and 5 touchdowns.

Although an injury and COVID-19 pandemic hampered the 2020 season, Kyle had his best career stats though playing in just 8 games. At the conclusion of the 2020 season, he had 43 receptions for 770 yards and 12 touchdowns.

Kyle Pitts Bio & Family; Age, Parents, Birthday, Height

The 20-years-of-age American football tight end was born as Kyle Anthony Pitts in 2000 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, the United States. He celebrates his birthday on 6th October every year. Kyle belongs to African-American ethnicity and his birth sign is Libra.

Kyle is not that vocal about his family’s whereabouts. However, we came to know from different sources that his mother’s name is Theresa Pitts.

About 6 feet and 6 inches (1.98 meters) tall in height, Kyle Pitts originally attended Abington Senior High School in Abington, Pennsylvania. Eventually, he transferred to Archbishop Wood Catholic High School in Warminster, Pennsylvania in 2016. Following that, he joined the University of Florida.
